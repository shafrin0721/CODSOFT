About Pettah

Pettah is a neighborhood located east of the city center Fort and behind the Colombo Port. It is renowned for the Pettah Market, a series of open-air bazaars and markets, making it one of Sri Lanka's busiest commercial areas. The market is designed like a gigantic crossword puzzle, allowing visitors to explore its many shops and stalls from dawn till dusk.

The name "Pettah" is derived from the Tamil word "Pettai," an Anglo-Indian term used to describe a suburb outside a fort. The Sinhala phrase "pita-kotuwa," meaning "outside the fort," aptly describes the area.
